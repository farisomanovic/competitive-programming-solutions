# XIV Cantonal IT Competition ZDK (2009) 🏆

This folder contains my solutions to the problems from the **XIV Cantonal IT Competition for High School Students**, held at the Mixed Secondary Technical School in Zavidovići, Bosnia and Herzegovina, on April 25, 2009.

## 📂 Problem Descriptions

Here are the translated descriptions of the five algorithmic tasks presented in the competition paper:

### Task 1: Number Interval Check
Write a program to enter three natural numbers a, b, and x. Check and print on the screen whether the number x is located in the interval [a,b], in the interval [1,a-1], or in the interval [b+1,32767]. The interval [a,b] includes the numbers a and b.
* **Example:** For a=6, b=10, x=8, the output is "x is in the interval [a,b]".

### Task 2: Sum of Squares and Cubes in an Interval
Write a program to load two natural numbers representing the upper and lower bounds of an interval. Calculate and print the sum consisting of the squares of even numbers and the cubes of odd numbers from the mentioned interval, including its boundaries. The maximum upper bound is 1000.
* **Example:** For n=2 and m=6, the sum is 208.

### Task 3: Largest Odd Divisor
Write a program to enter one **even** natural number n less than 32767. Print its largest odd divisor.
* **Example:** For n=60, the largest odd divisor of the number 60 is 15.

### Task 4: Trailing Zeros of a Product in an Interval
Write a program that loads the lower and upper bounds of a specific interval [n,m]. The bounds n and m are natural numbers. Print with how many zeros the product of all natural numbers from the mentioned interval ends, including its boundaries. The maximum upper bound is 32767.
* **Example:** For n=4 and m=10, the product of numbers from the interval [4,10] is 604800, and it ends with 2 zeros.

### Task 5: Closest Prime Number
Write a program where one natural number less than 32000 is entered. Print the prime number closest to it. There are three possibilities:
1. Print only one closest prime number.
2. There are two prime numbers equally distant from the read number, and both must be printed.
3. The entered number itself is a prime number, in which case print that same number.
*Note: Prime numbers are those that have only two divisors, the number 1 and themselves (e.g., 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17, 19...)*.
* **Example:** For input 18 the result is 17 and 19.
